Clarifying confusion in scRNA-seq analysis

The development of single cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) technologies has greatly facilitated investigation of important biological questions that were previously difficult or impossible to study. However, how to model and analyze scRNA-seq data has been the subject of considerable confusion and debate. We develop a simple way of thinking and talking about scRNA-seq data that can clarify much of this confusion. We outline a general framework which explicitly models, and distinguishes between, the measurement process and variation in actual expression levels between cells. Although our simple framework cannot on its own address all issues that arise in scRNA-seq data, we show how it can help clarify thinking, guide interpretation of existing analysis methods, address questions of biological interest, and suggest directions for development of new methods and analysis strategies.

Sarkar and Stephens, “Separating measurement and expression models clarifies confusion in single cell RNA-seq analysis.” BioRxiv 2020.
